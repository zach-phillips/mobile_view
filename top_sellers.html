10Best: Sellers

Ford F-Series (1)

Ford's ever-popular pickup truck lineup entered 2021 with a refreshed F-150 lineup that now includes a hybrid and an updated Super Duty model as well. Ford, and the F-150 in particular, has been hit hard by the global microchip shortage, and sales fell 8 percent YOY.

Ram Pickup (2)

Ram's truck lineup continues its ascent with a strong year that saw sales rise 1 percent. That was enough to beat the Silverado, which typically occupies the second-place sales ranking. Many attribute its rising sales to tame road manners and luxurious interiors.

Chevrolet Silverado (3)

Results for the Silverado were not great in 2021. It was outsold by the Ram truck lineup, and sales declined, with the light-duty model down nearly 13 percent and the HD version down nearly 8 percent. An updated lineup, including a fully-elecric model are imminent.

Toyota RAV4 (4)

Ever since it achieved the title of bestselling non-truck in the country, the RAV4 has been extending its lead over its compact-crossover rivals. Sales were down 5 percent compared with last year, mostly due to the ongoing pandemic and supply shortage.

Honda CR-V (5)

The CR-V is Honda's bread and butter, and its sales are up 9 percent over last year. A hybrid model joined the lineup recently, and we added one to our long-term test fleet. The SUV's ongoing popularity is due to its practical size and solid value-proposition.

Toyota Camry (6)

If there's one thing that's certain in the auto sales hierarchy, it's the Camry's place on top of the passenger-car ranks. The mid-size sedan experienced a 7 percent increase over last year, enough to beat out all of the entries on this list that are neither trucks nor SUVs.

Nissan Rogue (7)

The redesigned 2021 Rogue is far better than its predecessor, and the numbers are reflecting a similar improvement so far this year. Sales were up 25 percent compared with last year in spite of the ongoing pandemic; customers report paying higher prices than ever for Rogue models.

Jeep Grand Cherokee (8)

A new generation of the Grand Cherokee is now on sale, starting with the three-row L model, but the current two-row version is still available. A new two-row model is coming soon, but for now sales were up 26 percent compared with last year. A hybrid model is imminent.

Toyota Highlander (9)

The Highlander three-row SUV continues its ascent through the sales ranks in 2021 with a strong year. Sales were up 24 percent over last year. The Highlander remains one of the few credible, reliable, hybrid SUVs in the mainstream market.

Honda Civic (10)

The Civic was languishing in the first quarter of the year, but sales have risen since the redesigned 2022 Civic went on sale. Numbers were up 1 percent over last year. An all-new model brought better road manners and an upmarket interior to customers.
